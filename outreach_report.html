Background & Rationale

Girls and young women in underserved communities continue to face severe challenges:

  • Early pregnancies and school dropouts.
  • Gender-based vulnerabilities.
  • Limited livelihood opportunities and financial dependency.
  • Inadequate access to accurate Sexual and Reproductive Health Rights (SRHR) information.

The Arise & Community Outreach was designed to address these challenges by creating a safe, inclusive platform for learning, dialogue, and empowerment while strengthening community partnerships that support girls' development.

Objectives & Participants

Core Objectives

  • Provide accurate, youth-friendly SRHR information.
  • Build knowledge and skills in financial literacy.
  • Inspire confidence, leadership, and self-belief.
  • Promote menstrual health awareness and dignity.
  • Support vulnerable girls and young mothers with material donations.

Program Activities

1. Open Interaction: Girls and young mothers shared personal experiences and community challenges.

2. SRHR Awareness: Covered menstrual management, reproductive health, preventing early pregnancies/STIs, and debunking myths.

3. Financial Literacy: Introduced saving, budgeting, financial planning, and simple income-generating activities.

4. Motivational Session: Encouraged participants to overcome barriers and pursue education and leadership.

5. Period Party & Donations: Facilitated by Love Your Menses focusing on stigma reduction. Distributed reusable pads and clothing.

Slide 4 of 8

Outcomes & Needs Identified

Key Outcomes

  • Reached over 90 participants with integrated content.
  • Significantly improved understanding of SRHR.
  • Restored dignity for 75 girls through pad distribution.
  • Enhanced overall confidence and motivation.
  • Identified strong demand for hands-on skills training.

Community Needs Identified

During the sessions, participants highlighted specific areas requiring sustained support:

  • Practical vocational and hands-on skills training.
  • Continuous financial literacy and savings education.
  • Direct support for income-generating activities.
  • Mentorship and follow-up empowerment programs.

Conclusion & Recommendations

The Arise & Community Outreach successfully empowered adolescent girls and young mothers in Kanyogoga Community. It created a meaningful platform for education, support, and motivation.

Recommendations for the Future:

  • Implement hands-on vocational and skills training programs.
  • Conduct regular financial literacy and entrepreneurship sessions.
  • Strengthen mentorship and peer support structures.
  • Expand partnerships to support sustainable livelihoods.
