Our Journey & Vision

From Bukasa Girl Care Group to Visionary PathWays.

Our History

Visionary PathWays, formerly known as Bukasa Girl Care Group (BGCG), was initiated in October 2019 by a group of teenage girls and community elders concerned about the challenges facing disadvantaged urban girls. We were officially formed on August 7th, 2019, and registered on September 8th, 2021.


On our 5th anniversary in August 2024, we rebranded to Visionary PathWays to serve all young people aged 15 to 30 years, realizing that both boys and girls face critical economic and health challenges.

Our Vision

A generation of empowered, healthy, and self-reliant young people driving positive change in their communities.

Our Mission

Empowering young people with knowledge, skills, and opportunities in SRHR and entrepreneurship, enabling them to make informed decisions and build sustainable livelihoods.

Our Core Values

These values guide every decision we make:

Integrity Perseverance Accountability Authenticity Community

Our Objectives

  • Promote inclusive SRHR education.
  • Improve access to youth-friendly health services.
  • Empower youth through entrepreneurship training.
  • Promote gender equality and inclusion.
  • Strengthen leadership and life skills.

Our Key Projects

Real impact in Kanyogoga, Soweto, and Bukasa.

Reusable Pad Making

Training over 30 girls in the production of reusable sanitary pads to promote menstrual hygiene and economic skills.

SRHR Education

Providing age-appropriate sexual and reproductive health education to over 2,000 students in local schools.

'Wash for Her' Campaign

Raising funds and distributing Mama Kits to teenage mothers, ensuring safe and dignified childbirth.
