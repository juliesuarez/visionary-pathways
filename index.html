Our Journey & Vision

From Bukasa Girl Care Group to a comprehensive youth-empowerment NGO.

Our History

Visionary PathWays, formerly known as Bukasa Girl Care Group (BGCG), was initiated in October 2019 by a group of teenage girls and community elders concerned about the challenges facing disadvantaged urban girls in Bukasa, Kijwa zone, and Namuwongo. We were officially formed on August 7th, 2019, and registered on September 8th, 2021.

On our 5th anniversary in August 2024, recognizing that both boys and girls face critical economic and health challenges, we rebranded to Visionary PathWays to expand our reach to serve all young people aged 15 to 30 years.

Our Goal

To support young people through capacity-building activities, knowledge, skills, networks, and motivation that aim to improve living standards, dignity, and self-esteem.

Target Group

Youth, people with disabilities, teenage mothers, school dropouts, victims of gender-based violence, and vulnerable girls in the communities of Kanyogoga, Soweto, and surrounding areas.

Vision & Mission

Vision

A generation of empowered, healthy, and self-reliant young people driving positive change in their communities.

Mission

Empowering young people with knowledge, skills, and opportunities in sexual and reproductive health and rights (SRHR) and entrepreneurship, enabling them to make informed decisions and build sustainable livelihoods.

Our Objectives

  • Promote inclusive SRHR education by providing age-appropriate, gender-sensitive information.
  • Improve access to youth-friendly SRHR services ensuring safe, confidential care.
  • Empower youth through entrepreneurship building skills for self-reliance and economic growth.
  • Promote gender equality by advocating for equal opportunities and inclusion.
  • Strengthen leadership and life skills by nurturing confident, responsible youth leaders.

Our Core Programs

Targeted initiatives designed to uplift, educate, and empower the youth.

🤱

Rise Her Initiative

Supports teenage mothers through psychosocial support, SRHR education, entrepreneurship and vocational skills training, start-up capital support, mentorship, and linkages to supportive agencies to restore dignity and promote economic independence.

🚀

Rise Me Program

Empowers youth aged 15–30 through leadership development, SRHR education, entrepreneurship training, and digital engagement to foster the next generation of community leaders.

🏫

Pathfinders School

A structured school-based program implemented in primary and secondary schools focusing on age-appropriate SRHR, life skills, leadership training, mental health awareness, career guidance, and the formation of Visionary Clubs.

🎙️

Visionary Monthly Talks

Monthly interactive X Spaces where we invite leaders and experts to speak, inspire, and engage directly with young people on critical youth-centered topics and advocacy.

Impact & Achievements

Real, measurable change in Bukasa, Kanyogoga, and Soweto.

Vocational Skills Training

Trained over 30 girls in reusable sanitary pad making, and 20 girls in paper bag and briquette production, equipping them with eco-friendly skills for environmental sustainability and alternative livelihoods.

Maternal Health & Support

Raised UGX 370,000 in our 'Wash for Her, Raise Her' campaign and distributed 50 Mama Kits to teenage mothers to support safe deliveries. Provided start-up capital and school fees for teenage mothers.

Community Outreaches

Conducted 12 community outreaches focusing on SRHR and entrepreneurship. Official partners in the Annual Cloth Namuwongo Project (cleaning, tree planting, donation drives). Provided 20 bicycles to Community Health Workers.
